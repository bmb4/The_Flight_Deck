Rules

Four in a Sequence is a game played on a 7 column x 6 row board.
The object of the game is to get four of your color tokens in a continuous sequence in a straight line.
The line can be either left – right, up – down or diagonal.
The sequence in which the game is played follows this pattern:

1) One player will be randomly chosen to go first, players will alternate taking turns until game ends.
2) When it is your turn pick a column on which in which to place your token. Your token will occupy the lowest open space in the column.
3) Play continues until one player achieves four in a sequence or such a result is no longer possible.

Whichever player gets four in a sequence wins the game.
If neither player is able to get four in a row the game ends in a draw.

Strategies

Think ahead:
What is your opponent going to do in response to your move?
What will you do in response to theirs? The further you can plan ahead the better you can control the game.

Control the center:
The board is only 7 spaces wide which means any winning lines which are not vertical must go through the center column.

Play from the bottom up:
Remember that columns must be filled from the bottom line up.
Focus your attention on the lowest open spaces in each column.
Don’t worry too much about game ending moves that require spaces yet to be filled. Just remember not to fill them yourself.

Have multiple ways to win:
If you can get two rows of three at a time your opponent can only respond to one.
Ideally you should have one be completable if the other is blocked resulting in a guaranteed win.
Or have it so your opponent has the option of only blocking one of the two sequences.
